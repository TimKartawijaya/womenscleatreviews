About

We hope to see the gender gap in sports diminish.

Almost all soccer cleats on the market are designed to fit the feet of men, yet women account for a large amount of players looking for new cleats. Our goal is to help women navigate the difficult process of purchasing soccer cleats by providing useful information on men’s and women’s cleats that is specific to the needs of women and also push for the production of women’s soccer cleats.

Our Story

In 2016 my little sister had knee surgery.


While playing soccer she tore her mPCL. Before surgery the doctor assured her that knee injuries were common among soccer players and even more common among female soccer players. So I asked myself, “is this true and if it is, then what is the soccer industry doing about it?”

Isabella made varsity her freshman year of high school and had walked on to her club team the year before that. At the start of her freshman year she even decided to run cross country on a whim. Her first season she became the second best runner on the team.

How then could an athlete of this caliber get injured? Well the simple answer is that all athletes get injured eventually.

Yet as I dug deeper, my research discovered that female soccer players have knee injuries 6 times more frequently than their male counterparts!

Months after her recovery my sister asked me to help her buy new indoor soccer cleats. When I started looking I couldn’t find any soccer cleats made for women. All I found were men’s cleats with the occasional women’s cleats. If women and men have different body mechanics, then shouldn’t they be wearing different cleats?

That’s where we come in.
